BICEPS
The biceps are muscles on the front part of the upper arm. The bicep includes a short head and a long head that work as a single muscle. The bicep is attached to the arm bones by tough connective tissues called tendons. The tendons that connect the bicep muscle to the shoulder joint in two places are called the proximal biceps tendons. The tendon that attaches the bicep muscle to the forearm bones (radius and ulna) is called the distal biceps tendon. When the bicep contracts, it pulls the forearm up and rotates it outward.
